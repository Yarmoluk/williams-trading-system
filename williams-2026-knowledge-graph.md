## Williams Indicator Hierarchy (Decision Flow)

### Layer 1: COT Index (Directional Bias — Weekly)
- Commercial net position normalized 0-100 over 3-year lookback
- Formula: (Current Net - 3yr Low) / (3yr High - 3yr Low) x 100
- Buy: Commercial Index > 75 AND Large Speculator Index < 25
- Sell: Commercial Index < 25 AND Large Speculator Index > 75
- Commercials are the "smart money" — they hedge real business exposure

### Layer 2: Seasonal Pattern (Confirmation)
- 15-30 year average return for current calendar period
- Requires 65%+ historical consistency to confirm
- Seasonal + COT alignment = Williams' highest conviction setup

### Layer 3: Williams Accumulation/Distribution (Trend Confirmation)
- Price-only indicator — does NOT use volume
- Up day: Close - Min(Low, Prior Close)
- Down day: Close - Max(High, Prior Close)
- Cumulative running sum
- Divergence from price = early reversal warning
- Bullish: price falling but A/D rising (accumulation despite weakness)

### Layer 4: Williams %R (Entry Trigger)
- Formula: (Highest High - Close) / (Highest High - Lowest Low) x -100
- Default period: 14
- Range: 0 to -100
- Overbought: above -20 | Oversold: below -80
- DO NOT simply buy at oversold — use the Failure Swing method:
  - Buy: %R drops below -80, rises above -80, pulls back but stays above -80, continues higher
  - Sell: %R rises above -20, drops below -20, rallies but stays below -20, continues lower

### Layer 4 (Alt): Ultimate Oscillator (Entry Trigger)
- Williams invented this in 1985
- Three periods: 7, 14, 28 weighted 4:2:1
- Formula: UO = 100 x (4xAvg7 + 2xAvg14 + 1xAvg28) / 7
- Buy signal (ALL three required): bullish divergence + UO low below 30 + UO breaks above interim high
- Sell signal (ALL three required): bearish divergence + UO high above 70 + UO breaks below interim low
- Exit longs when UO > 70 | Exit shorts when UO < 30

### Layer 5: Pattern Detection (Entry Signal)

#### OOPS! Pattern (~86% win rate in bonds)
- Long: today's open < yesterday's low → buy stop at yesterday's low
- Short: today's open > yesterday's high → sell stop at yesterday's high
- Exit: first profitable open (close at next day's open if profitable)

#### Smash Day
- Buy setup: close below previous day's low + wide range → buy stop 1 tick above smash day's high
- Sell setup: close above previous day's high + wide range → sell stop 1 tick below smash day's low
- Must NOT be an outside bar

#### Volatility Breakout
- Buy level: Open + (0.25 x Yesterday's Range)
- Sell level: Open - (0.25 x Yesterday's Range)
- Target: 2x the breakout spread | Stop: 2x the breakout spread
- Best after narrow range days (compression before expansion)

#### Inside Day
- Today's range contained within yesterday's range
- Signals compression → trade the breakout direction
- Continuation pattern

#### Outside Day
- Today's range engulfs yesterday's range
- Close near high = bullish reversal | Close near low = bearish reversal

### Layer 6: Position Sizing (Execution)
- Fixed fractional: 2% max risk per trade
- Stop at prior swing point
- Contracts = (Account x 0.02) / (Stop distance x point value)
- ES futures: $50 per point

## Key Rules
1. Cycles tell you WHEN, not HOW FAR — use them for timing, not price targets
2. Buy market breaks — don't try to pick the exact bottom
3. No single indicator is a system — layer them for confluence
4. Failure swings > simple overbought/oversold
5. 2% max risk per trade — always
6. Hold winners — "You will never make big money until you learn to hold on to your winners"
7. Exit longs at UO > 70 | Exit shorts at UO < 30
